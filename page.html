Explore the Skies: A Guide to Cloud Types








From the fluffy to wispy, each cloud type has a story to tell.. Whether you're a weather enthusiast, a student, or simply curious, understanding the beauty and science of clouds can help you better appreciate the world around you.

Categories of Cloud Types

Clouds are formed when water vapor in the air condenses into water droplets. There are several types of clouds, each with different characteristics. Below are listed the 4 main categories.

  1. Cirro-form

      2. High-altitude clouds that form above 20,000 feet and are composed of ice crystals due to the cold temperatures at that altitude.

  2. Cumulo-form

      3. Fluffy, white clouds with a flat base that form at low to middle altitudes. They are often associated with fair weather but can develop into cumulonimbus clouds (storm-producing clouds)

  3. Strato-form

      4. Low-altitude clouds in continuous layers/sheets covering the sky, often associated with stable weather conditions

  4. Nimbo-form

      5. Clouds that produce precipitation, bringing continuous, steady rain or snow or can be associated with thunderstorms and can produce heavy rainfall, lightning, and hail.
